U.S. Title 34

Crime Control and Law Enforcement

Provisions from Titles 18, 28, 42 were reclassified and coded to the new Crime Control and Law Enforcement title. Some of the provisions moved included The Omnibus Crime Control Act of 1968 and the Sex Offender Registration Notification Act (SORNA). The other provisions not reclassified were left in the original title because they were better retained there. Though both Title 18 and 34 engage in the criminal system, Title 18 is the procedure of the criminal system while Title 34 deals with crime control.

Terminology
Non-Positive Law: Title of the code consisting of an Editorial Arrangement of Federal Statutes.
Positive Law:Title of the code that has been enacted into law.
Editorial Classification: Process of organizing laws into sections of the code.
U.S. Code:Complitation of general and permanent Laws in the United States.
Provisions: A section or statutory note that is based on a federal statute.
Reclassification: process of moving provisions from one place to another in the code, while keeping statutory text at the same time.
Omnibus: A volume containing several novels or other items previously published separately
